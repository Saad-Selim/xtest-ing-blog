## Understanding Quick Sanity Checks

Quick Sanity Checks, often referred to as sanity testing, is a narrow and deep approach to software testing. Its primary purpose is to determine whether the changes introduced in a particular function or module have not adversely impacted the functionality of the software.

### Why Perform Quick Sanity Checks?

With the rise of Agile and DevOps practices, software development and deployment cycles have dramatically shortened. According to a [Statista survey](https://www.statista.com/statistics/832685/worldwide-developer-survey-frequency-of-deployments/), approximately 14% of developers deploy multiple times per day, and 27% deploy about once per day. In such a fast-paced environment, Quick Sanity Checks can provide a speedy verification method to ensure that new features or bug fixes have not broken the existing functionalities of the software.

## Real-World Applications and Benefits of Quick Sanity Checks

Quick Sanity Checks have wide-ranging applications across various domains and industries. Whether it's a banking application, an e-commerce platform, or a healthcare software, sanity checks can quickly validate the software's functionality after a minor update or fix. Here are some of the key benefits:

*   **Time-saving:** Instead of running a full regression test, sanity checks focus on the changed functionalities, saving considerable time.
*   **Cost-effective:** By catching bugs early in the development cycle, sanity checks can reduce the cost of bug fixes.
*   **Improved software quality:** By ensuring that the new changes have not broken existing functionalities, sanity checks can significantly improve the overall software quality.
