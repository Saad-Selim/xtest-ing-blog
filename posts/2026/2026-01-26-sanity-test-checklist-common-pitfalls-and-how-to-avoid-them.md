## What is a Sanity Test?

A sanity test is a narrow and deep approach to software testing, aimed at determining whether a particular function of the software is working as it should after a minor update. Unlike regression tests, sanity tests do not cover all functionalities but focus on the affected components.

### Significance of Sanity Testing

Sanity testing plays a vital role in the software development lifecycle, as it helps detect bugs or defects before the software is released. It ensures that the changes introduced do not adversely affect the software's existing functionalities. The testing process, therefore, improves the software's overall quality and reliability.

## Creating a Robust Sanity Test Checklist

A sanity test checklist is a list of critical functions that must pass the sanity test. Here's a step-by-step guide to crafting an effective sanity test checklist using Xtest:

### 1\. Identify Critical Functions

Identify the critical functions of your software. These are the features that your software cannot do without and should be working flawlessly at all times. Include these functions in your sanity test checklist.

### 2\. Prioritize Based on Recent Changes

After identifying the critical functions, prioritize them based on the recent changes in your software. The features that have been recently modified or updated should be tested first.
