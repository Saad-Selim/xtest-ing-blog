## Understanding Automated Smoke Testing

Before we dive into the practicalities, let's first understand what automated smoke testing is. In software engineering, smoke testing, also known as "build verification testing," is a type of software testing that comprises a non-exhaustive set of tests to ensure the most crucial functions work. The term originated from the hardware testing practice of turning on a new piece of hardware for the first time and considering it a success if it does not catch fire.

In the context of software development, smoke testing is a rapid way to catch glaring errors and problems. Manual smoke testing can be time-consuming and prone to human error. Here's where automated smoke testing comes into play, offering a faster, more reliable way to verify that your software's basic functionality works as expected.

## Why Choose Automated Smoke Test Suites?

### 1\. Speed and Efficiency

Automated smoke testing can execute a broad range of tests in a short time, providing rapid feedback to developers and reducing the time to market. For instance, [Software Testing Help](https://www.softwaretestinghelp.com/smoke-testing/) reports that automation can execute tests 10 to 20 times faster than manual testing.

### 2\. Accuracy

Unlike manual testing, automated smoke tests are not prone to human error, ensuring accurate and reliable results every time. This means you'll have a more reliable product and fewer headaches down the line.

### 3\. Reusability

Automated smoke tests are reusable, which means they can be easily replicated across different versions of the software or used to test other components within the same application.
