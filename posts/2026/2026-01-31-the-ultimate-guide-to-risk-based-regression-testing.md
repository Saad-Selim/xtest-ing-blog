## What is Risk-Based Regression Testing?

Risk-based regression testing is an approach to software testing that prioritizes tests based on the potential risks associated with each part of the software. This strategy is particularly useful when resources are limited and you need to make sure that the most critical parts of your software are thoroughly tested.

### How Does It Work?

In risk-based regression testing, software features and functions are ranked based on their potential risk. This risk can be determined by factors like the complexity of the feature, the likelihood of failure, and the impact of a potential failure. The highest-risk features are then tested first, ensuring that any major issues are caught as early as possible.

## The Importance of Risk-Based Regression Testing

Today's software development environment is characterized by rapid changes and frequent releases. As a result, regression testing has become increasingly important. According to a report by the National Institute of Standards and Technology, inadequate testing of software costs the U.S. economy nearly $60 billion annually. Among these costs, the most significant are those associated with fixing defects after release.

Risk-based regression testing is a way to tackle this challenge head-on. By identifying and focusing on the areas of your software that pose the greatest risk, you can allocate your testing resources more effectively and prevent costly post-release fixes.
