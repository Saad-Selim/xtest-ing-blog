## What is WCAG 2.1 Compliance?

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 are a set of guidelines developed by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) to ensure that web content is accessible to all users, including those with visual, hearing, cognitive, and physical impairments. WCAG 2.1 compliance means that a website or application meets these guidelines, providing a more inclusive and accessible user experience.

## Why is WCAG 2.1 Compliance Important?

Accessibility is not only a moral obligation - it's also a legal requirement in many jurisdictions. According to the Bureau of Internet Accessibility, over 1000 lawsuits related to digital accessibility were filed in the U.S. in 2020 alone. By ensuring WCAG 2.1 compliance, businesses can avoid costly litigation, while also reaching a broader audience and improving their brand image.
